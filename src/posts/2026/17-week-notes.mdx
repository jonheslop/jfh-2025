Ok so third week note of the year... I’ve managed the regular cadence of week 2, 8 and now 17, not great. The prod for to write this week was the huge shock of seeing my name in [Phil’s list](https://www.gyford.com/phil/writing/2026/04/21/blogroll-keepers-11/) on Tuesday, thanks Phil!

## House

Ok so this year is hurtling, we’re keeping ourselves busy between work, DIY, the allotment and trying to move house, which is getting increasingly complex. Working with solicitors is hard, slow and boring. You’d imagine they’d offer help or advice but I suppose you have to pay a lot more for that sort of thing. Anyway we plod on, I’m hoping soon have some breakthrough moment soon.

Loft DIY is almost done, it’s really nice up there, we don’t plan to move up  so it’s basically now a very large and nice room to do Zwift in, it’s got a good view, you can just about see Alexandra Palace. The only job left to do is sand and oil the stairs which is going to be long.

## Allotment

Now the weather is picking up it’s increasingly nice to spend time down the allotment, the only crop we’re getting at the moment is asparagus, but that’s very satisfying, every 3 or so days we go cut more off, it’s something you can appreciate the freshness of. We’ve added some more beds and have tamed a lot of the overgrown blackberry and raspberry bushes.

Whilst we were in Wales, our friends Colin and Emma looked after it, so now we’re sharing the burden/bounty together. On the weekend Emma’s parents came to visit from Orkney and her dad, a retired farmer wanted to get stuck in. So now it’s even tidier which is brill. Was nice to meet them, proper good accents.

We have a automatic watering system by [Harvst](https://harvst.io/) in our greenhouse, it had stopped working we sent it back and paid £20 and they refurbed it and sent a new pump. So now it’s up and running watering our seedlings twice a day. Soon we’ll plant out the tomatoes and it really comes into it’s own.

## TV

In my [last notes](/posts/2026/10-week-notes/) I mentioned TV. We finished _How to Get to Heaven from Belfast_ maybe it didn’t quite land, I wouldn’t call it a let down but I don’t think I’ve really thought about it a lot since, was still enjoyable. We sped through series three of _Industry_, I was familiar with the Wirecard scandal (thanks to [TrashFuture](https://trashfuture.co.uk/) mainly) but that wasn’t really necessary. It is quite the intense watch, but I really enjoy the soundtrack. The characters are all quite unredeemably unlikeable. I did feel sorry for Henry by the end. Yasmin on the other hand just gets more and more of a monster. Bec did question whether the series as a whole is just shock factor and not actually good... perhaps but I still liked it. It’s also fun how close it tracks to real UK events (e.g. like a new flailing Labour government).
