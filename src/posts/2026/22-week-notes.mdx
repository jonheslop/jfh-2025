## ESP32 devices

[The Poem/1 clock](https://poem.town/) (excellent <span className="small-caps">TLD</span>) by [Matt Webb](https://interconnected.org/) arrived on Tuesday, it’s a nice object and it’s fun to look at it. The poems themselves are mostly silly and kinda cringe, but I that sorta feels like how it should be. You can press a button on the top of the clock to fave poems. Here’s a few I saved:

> Ten seventeen, the cosmos gleams, / In vastness where we weave our dreams.

Deep...

> A raven caws at three oh three, / Its shadow whispers, ‘Come and see.’

I like ravens! Although feels a bit dark

> At five oh five, a sigh, / A leaf drifts down, goodbye.

Is this melancholy?

After I [read Matt’s blogpost](https://interconnected.org/home/2026/05/20/resident) about [Resident](https://resident.inanimate.tech/), I bought myself a [M5StickS3 from PiHut](https://thepihut.com/products/m5sticks3-esp32s3-mini-iot-dev-kit) and it arrived on Thursday. Whilst I was trying to set it up I inadvertently bricked the poem clock — it was also plugged into my Mac (via my screen) and I didn’t realise it was also a ESP32 device so when I ran the command line tool to flash the stick it actually flashed the clocks firmware, the clocks e ink display is now stuck showing 10:52 forever. I got in touch with Matt thinking I could reimage it myself, but he says it’s more complicated so I’m going to have to visit him at his lab. 

After my error I did get the stick up and running. I’ve made it into a [PR notifier](https://github.com/jonheslop/pr-fanfare/). A Cloudflare worker polls GitHub every two minutes, sends out updates over websockets and the stick beeps out a little fanfare when there’s a new PR. It worked well today, GitHub doesn’t make this easy I find, and I like to keep my mac fairly notification free so this feels like a nice more ambient/calm way to keep up.
