## The heat

Week two of the mega pan-European heatwave, it started off still fairly novel and I felt we were just about keeping on top of things, with lots of shades, strategic opening/closing of windows, use of fans and using the portable aircon unit as a backstop.

But by Wednesday it was getting pretty unbareable, the fabric of our house was so thoroughly warmed that we couldn’t really get things much lower than 29ºC before midnight. Wednesday, Thursday and Friday we slept with the aircon on all night (and wore earplugs to make it bearable), luckily our sash windows work pretty well with it, it’s demoralising how once you switch it off it immediately feels awful again.

## Passivhaus tour

We’ve always been interested in [Passivhaus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passive_house) and the heat this week was quite radicalising. We’re seriously thinking about aiming for the EnerPHit standard for the new house, it will a lot of work but surely worth it? With that in mind Bec spotted an OpenHouse weekend event at a new build Passivhaus in Canonbury so we went there on Saturday with our friend (and architect) Ulf to tour the house and see if we could learn anything.

It’s good hearing about someone else’s experience of it all, this was a house designed by an architect for his mother for her retirement,  it looked single storey but had three bedrooms in the basement and was very impressive. It took _six (6) years_ to get it through planning... insane.
