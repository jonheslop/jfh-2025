On Monday I cycled to Stratford to give blood, my 36th time, it’s all worth it for the free Seabrook crips and mint Club chocolate bar.

I went into the office Tuesday with Maude, we walked from Green Park which was nice. A colleague’s puppy also came in, Maude acted aloof all morning ignoring them I guess to assert her authority or something but it fell apart by lunch and they spent the afternoon wrestling/play fighting. Which is cute but a bit distracting for everyone.

My parents flew back from LA and stayed on Tuesday evening, we had an early dinner and then we got up at 5:30 so I could drive them home before working from their house for the day, after work I popped over to see my Nan for a quick catch up and then walked around the corner to see my aunt and uncle too.

On Thursday I went to the office again, twice in one week... hasn’t really happened much since the pandemic, but the new office is much better and I’m trying to make more of an effort — plus it’s airconditioned which makes it a lot more comfortable that home in this heat. Maude spent a lot of time wrestling with Barley again. I met Bec and Nat at Coal Drops after work and we sat on the lawns by the canal all evening.

Saturday was my birthday and Bec took me for lunch at [Teal](https://www.tealbysallyabe.com), it was really good. I’m a fan of small plates but it was nice to just have y’know classic starter, main, dessert for a change. After, we moseyed around Hackney for the afternoon, before succumbing to the heat and retreating inside the Barbican to recoup (and play [Regenwormen](https://www.intertoys.nl/tiny-tins-regenwormen)) before heading over to meet a bunch of friends at the pub for the evening. Was nice to catch up with everyone and Germany won, so Ulf was happy.

On Sunday we mainly hid from the heat, but did spend some time at the allotment sorting bits out. It’s mad how quick things are growing now. Soon we’ll have more peas, potatoes, cucumbers, tomatoes, courgettes and raspberries than we know what to do with.

We also finished watching [Legends](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt33265765/) which I enjoyed, the story is based on true events and it’s mad, I knew nothing about it.
